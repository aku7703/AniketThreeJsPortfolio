Hello everyone! I'm Aniket Chaudhry, and I recently had the incredible opportunity to represent Northeastern University at the **University Nanosatellite Program Mission Concept Kickoff** event at NASA's Kennedy Space Center. Alongside my dedicated teammate Jacob Oshinsky, and with the support of Andrew Benincasa and Sergi Aliaga from the Ultrabroadband Nanonetworking Laboratory, we embarked on a journey that was both educational and inspiring.

## Presenting THIS-SAT

At the event, we had the privilege of presenting our THIS-SAT satellite mission to an audience of professionals from:
- NASA
- Air Force Office of Scientific Research (AFOSR)
- Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL/RV)
- Space Dynamics Laboratory (SDL)

It was an exhilarating experience to stand before experts in the field and share the work we have been so passionate about. Our mission focuses on advancing satellite systems engineering and pushing the boundaries of what's possible in space exploration.

## Training & Experiences

One of the highlights of the event was the intensive satellite systems engineering training we received. This training was invaluable as it provided us with the tools and knowledge to create well-scoped, feasible, and meaningful satellite missions. The skills we acquired are already being put to use during our internships this summer.

Beyond the formal presentations and training, we had the unique opportunity to explore areas of Kennedy Space Center that are not normally accessible to the public. Walking through NASA's Vehicle Assembly Building (VAB) and visiting several cutting-edge laboratories was nothing short of awe-inspiring.

## Looking Forward

I am incredibly proud of the work we did and the experiences we gained at the University Nanosatellite Program Mission Concept Kickoff event. We are part of something truly special, and I can't wait to see where our efforts will take us next.

Join me in celebrating the achievements of our team and the bright future of space exploration. **The sky is not the limit – it's just the beginning.**

Stay curious and keep reaching for the stars!

*-Aniket Chaudhry*
